Where in Fulham?

Every corner of SW6 has its own character

Fulham isn't one place — it's six or seven distinct villages that happen to share a postcode. Here's how to find yours.

Why Fulham

London living without the London compromise

Fulham sits in a rare position — close enough to Chelsea and Kensington to feel central, far enough to still have streets you can actually afford.

🚇
Three tube stations
Fulham Broadway, Parsons Green and Putney Bridge on the District line. Sloane Square in under 10 minutes.
🌊
The Thames on your doorstep
Bishop's Park stretches along the riverbank with towpath walks all the way to Hammersmith and Putney.
🏡
Victorian stock in abundance
Terraced houses, mansion flats and garden squares — more period character per street than almost anywhere in Zone 2.
📈
Consistent price growth
SW6 has outperformed the London average over every 5-year period since 2000. Buyers who stretched have never regretted it.

Parks & Green Space

1
Bishop's Park
Fulham's crown jewel — 27 acres on the Thames with an ornamental lake, playgrounds, tennis courts and the historic Fulham Palace.
Thames-side
2
Parsons Green
The triangular village green at the heart of SW6's social scene. Ringed by pubs, it's packed every summer weekend.
Village green
3
Eel Brook Common
A proper local park with tennis, basketball, a dog run and a playground. The heart of Munster Village life.
Local park
4
Hurlingham Park
Beautiful 42-acre park in Sands End, adjoining the exclusive Hurlingham Club. Family-friendly and rarely crowded.
42 acres
5
Thames Towpath
Walk or cycle from Putney Bridge to Hammersmith along one of London's finest riverside routes.
Thames-side
